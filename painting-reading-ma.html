Exterior Painting in Reading, MA

4 miles from Wakefield — expert exterior painting for Reading's diverse homes

Reading shares a border with Wakefield, and we have been painting homes here since the beginning. The town has a distinctive mix of housing that keeps our work varied: cedar-shingled colonials near Reading Common, newer garrison colonials and contemporary homes in the Birch Meadow area, and established neighborhoods of capes and ranches along the streets off Main Street toward the Woburn line. Each material and era demands a different prep and paint strategy, and we bring that knowledge to every project.

Cedar shingles are one of the most common exterior surfaces we encounter in Reading. When maintained properly, cedar weathers beautifully — but when it is neglected, it splits, cups, and turns gray. We prep cedar by power washing at the right pressure (too high and you damage the wood), replacing shingles that have failed, and applying solid or semi-transparent stain depending on the homeowner's preference. Clapboard-sided homes near the town center get a different treatment: scraping, spot-priming, caulking, and two coats of premium exterior paint. The point is that we do not use a one-size-fits-all approach. Your home's surfaces dictate the process.

Reading's commercial properties along Haven Street and the shops near the Reading Depot also benefit from our exterior painting services. We work around business hours, prep and mask carefully to protect storefronts, and use durable Sherwin-Williams coatings built for high-traffic commercial surfaces. Whether it is a single-family home backing up to the Ipswich River, a two-family near downtown, or a commercial building on Main Street, we deliver the same careful workmanship on every job.
